Carcassonne Rules

  1. At the beginning of the game, the player receives 4 random tiles.
  2. The player can only play one tile per turn.
  3. With each new turn, the player receives a new random tile to replace the one used in the previous turn.
  4. The player has the option to replace their tiles with 4 new ones using the discard button, this button is activated after 5 turns, and after use, it must be played for 5 turns again to be activated.
  5. There are 3 types of tiles in the game: Road, City, and Abbey.
  6. The board starts with a road tile in the center of the board.
  7. Road tiles can only be placed adjacent to another road tile, and each tile placed equals one point on the score counter.
  8. City and Abbey tiles can be placed adjacent to road, city, or abbey tiles. City tiles award 2 points per tile and if placed adjacent to another city tile, each tile in the city chain awards an additional 3 points.
  9. Abbey tiles award 1 point for each tile adjacent to its perimeter, so a maximum of 8 points can be scored if the abbey tile is completely surrounded by other tiles.
  10. The game ends when there are no empty spaces on the board or when no more moves are possible, and the option to discard tiles is not available
